# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Three overlapping reader groups (documented in the pipeline's writer stage prompt):
1. Neurodiverse readers (ADHD, dyslexia) who bounce off walls of text.
2. Older / non-technical readers new to the tech world.
3. People who already know a topic but want it explained better than the docs do.

Context: casual reading, often arriving from an Instagram carousel (@litbulblab). One post per day is published automatically at midnight by an AI pipeline (research → write → illustrate → edit → publish).

## Product Purpose
An accessibility-first explainer blog ("Hard things, drawn simple") covering AI, Tech, Trading, and Tinkering. Success = readers actually finish posts, come back for new ones, and follow the Instagram → site funnel. The site is also the proof-of-concept for a fully automated nightly content pipeline.

## Brand Personality
Warm, calm, quietly funny. Editorial-diagram visual language (warm paper palette, single #d6552b orange accent, Fraunces + Atkinson Hyperlegible), "a little flashy but never busy." Never condescending, never breathless.

## Anti-references
- **AI-generated genericism** — interchangeable landing pages, gradient heroes, identical card grids. This site is made BY an AI pipeline; looking AI-made would be fatal to trust.
- **Dense hacker aesthetic** — HN/lobste.rs walls of text, monospace-everything, zero visual explanation.
- **Noisy/gamified edu sites** — mascots, badges, streaks, confetti; patronizing for adult readers.

## Distribution constraint (affects design decisions)
New-post announcement currently depends entirely on manually posting an Instagram carousel (slides generated by the pipeline to ~/Desktop/litbulb-instagram/). This step is manual, has broken tooling (browser file-upload failures), and previous Graph API automation attempts died on token exchange/expiry. Until that's fixed, the site itself carries the full burden of surfacing new content to returning visitors — it currently does none of this (no latest-posts section, no RSS, no dates on the homepage).

## Design Principles
1. **Practice what you preach** — a site about visual, calm explanation must itself be visual and calm; every index page should show, not tell.
2. **One idea per screen** — applies to marketing surfaces too, not just posts.
3. **Freshness must be visible** — a daily-publishing site that looks static kills its own return-visit loop.
4. **The picture comes first** — posts lead with imagery; index surfaces should too.
5. **Accessible is the brand, not a checkbox** — Atkinson Hyperlegible, 18px base, short measure, dark mode, reduced motion are already committed; never regress them.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA minimum. Known needs: ADHD (short chunks, single focus), dyslexia (Atkinson Hyperlegible, loose leading, short measure), low vision (18px base, high contrast), reduced motion honored. Dark mode via prefers-color-scheme already shipped.
